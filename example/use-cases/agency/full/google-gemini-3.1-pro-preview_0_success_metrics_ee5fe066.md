# Outcome Alignment
All technical integrations—including the real-time Toast-to-Shopify inventory sync, the automated fresh batch email alerts, and the dynamic pricing engine—are designed specifically to increase daily inventory sell-through. By unifying the physical and digital storefronts, this architecture improves customer retention through transparency and hyper-relevant notifications, while simultaneously automating manual operations. Ultimately, these capabilities align directly with the bakery's core profitability goals and aim to significantly reduce end-of-day food waste.



# North Star Metric
### Daily Sell-Through Rate

The overarching measure of success for this digital transformation is the **Daily Sell-Through Rate**, defined as the percentage of produced baked goods sold by the end of each operational day. 

*   **Target**: Consistently achieve a >95% daily sell-through rate across all perishable inventory.
*   **Rationale**: Maximizing this metric demonstrates that real-time inventory visibility, timely fresh batch alerts, and automated late-day pricing discounts are actively capturing immediate customer demand and minimizing lost revenue.



# Primary KPIs
The following primary KPIs will be tracked to validate the effectiveness of the integrated solution:

*   **Omnichannel Order Volume**: Total volume of orders processed across in-store (Toast) and digital (Shopify) channels. *Target: 20% year-over-year growth in total daily orders.*
*   **Inventory Sync Discrepancy Rate (%)**: The variance between physical stock and digital storefront availability. *Target: < 1% discrepancy.* (Zero online orders should be refunded due to out-of-stock issues).
*   **Email Conversion Rate (Fresh Notifications)**: The percentage of customers who place an order after receiving a 'Fresh Batch' notification. *Target: 10%+ conversion within 2 hours of dispatch.*
*   **Customer Review Sentiment Score**: Aggregate metric reflecting customer satisfaction, particularly regarding freshness and availability. *Target: 4.5/5.0 average rating on integrated WordPress reviews.*



# Leading Indicators
Early signals that the automated features and digital storefront are driving desired behaviors include:

*   **Email Open and Click-Through Rates**: High engagement (>25% open rate) on fresh batch notifications indicates strong customer interest and list health.
*   **Website Traffic Surges**: Immediate, measurable spikes in WordPress/Shopify concurrent visitors following the dispatch of fresh batch notifications.
*   **Add-to-Cart Rates During Automated Discount Hours**: Increased cart activity in the final 2 hours of operation, validating the automated dynamic pricing guidance engine's effectiveness at moving day-old stock.



# Lagging Indicators
These metrics will be evaluated on a monthly or quarterly basis to confirm sustained business impact:

*   **Total Monthly Revenue**: Overall financial growth driven by the combination of physical and new digital sales streams.
*   **Reduction in Monthly Food Waste (COGS)**: Measured by the decrease in the cost of goods sold attributed to unsold, discarded inventory. *Target: 15% reduction in daily unsold food waste.*
*   **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)**: The total revenue generated by a customer over their relationship with the bakery, expected to rise due to higher retention and personalization.



# Guardrails
To ensure the system performs reliably without degrading the customer experience, the following constraints must be strictly maintained:

*   **Middleware API Error Rate**: Must remain **< 0.1%** to guarantee accurate inventory and pricing synchronization between Toast and Shopify.
*   **Email Unsubscribe Rate**: Must not exceed **1% per campaign**; breaching this indicates notification fatigue or poor targeting logic.
*   **WordPress Core Web Vitals (LCP)**: Largest Contentful Paint must remain **< 2.5 seconds** to prevent high bounce rates on the frontend.



# Measurement Plan
The monitoring and measurement strategy leverages best-in-class operational and marketing tools to capture a holistic view of system health and user behavior:

*   **Frontend & E-commerce Tracking**: Implement Google Analytics 4 (GA4) with cross-domain and e-commerce tracking across the WordPress frontend and Shopify checkout.
*   **Middleware & Infrastructure**: Utilize AWS CloudWatch to monitor API Gateway requests, Lambda execution times, and Redis cache hit rates. Custom metrics will be configured for sync events.
*   **Marketing & Engagement**: Leverage the Email Service Provider's (ESP) built-in analytics dashboard to track campaign performance (opens, clicks, conversions).
*   **Data Aggregation**: All critical metrics will be aggregated weekly into a centralized BI tool or custom dashboard for cross-functional visibility.



# Risk Signals
The following warning signs indicate potential system failures or process breakdowns, requiring immediate investigation:

*   **API Timeouts or 429 Errors**: Frequent `429 Too Many Requests` or timeout errors from Toast or Shopify APIs, indicating rate limit breaches or upstream outages.
*   **Customer Service Escalations**: A sudden spike in support emails or in-store complaints regarding missing online inventory, resulting in poor customer experience.
*   **Checkout Abandonment Rate**: Rates exceeding **60%**, which may suggest friction in the Shopify checkout flow or unexpected fulfillment/pickup constraints.



# Next Steps
To operationalize this metrics framework, the following actions must be prioritized:

1.  **Establish Baselines**: Extract current offline sales, waste logs, and customer return rates to set historical benchmarks.
2.  **Tagging & Instrumentation**: Configure GA4 properties, Shopify pixel integrations, and set up custom event tracking for 'Add to Cart' and 'Fresh Batch' triggers.
3.  **Provision Monitoring Tools**: Deploy application performance monitoring (APM) and alerting tools, specifically Sentry for frontend/middleware error tracking and Datadog/CloudWatch for infrastructure health.

# Reporting Cadence
Reporting will be structured across three operational tiers to serve different stakeholders:

*   **Real-Time**: Automated alerts for technical failures, API sync drops, or critical system thresholds.
*   **Weekly**: Summary reports delivered to operational stakeholders (Bakery Managers, Marketers) focusing on sell-through rates, inventory sync health, and campaign performance.
*   **Monthly**: Deep-dive reviews for executive and strategic planning, analyzing lagging indicators like revenue growth, waste reduction, and CLV changes.



# Ownership
Clear accountability ensures metrics are continuously monitored and actionable:

*   **Product Owner**: Accountable for tracking and improving business-level KPIs (Daily Sell-Through Rate, Omnichannel Revenue, Email Conversion Rate).
*   **Technical Lead**: Owns system guardrails, infrastructure uptime, middleware API error rates, and the accuracy/latency of the Toast-to-Shopify inventory synchronization.



# Escalation Plan
Standard operating procedures for metric breaches to minimize business disruption:

*   **Technical Escalation**: If the API synchronization failure between Toast and Shopify exceeds 5 minutes, an automated critical alert will page the on-call engineering team via PagerDuty for immediate triage and remediation.
*   **Marketing Escalation**: If email conversion metrics drop by 20% week-over-week, or if unsubscribe rates breach the 1% guardrail, automated alerts will notify the marketing team to pause automated campaigns and comprehensively review targeting and frequency logic.
